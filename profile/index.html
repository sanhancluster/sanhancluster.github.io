Research Interests

High-resolution zoom-in simulation of a galaxy cluster

Han et al. (in prep, 2024a)

I'm a builder of NewCluster simulation, a cosmological hydrodynamic zoom-in simulation that aims to reproduce a galaxy cluster with very high resolution (~70 pc). The simulation will cover a region of galaxy cluster with a virial mass of 500 trillion solar masses and include baryonic prescriptions of star formation and stellar feedback, formation and activity of supermassive black holes, evolution of chemical species and dust.

Improving astrophysical simulation code RAMSES (Han et al. in prep, 2023a)

Han et al. (in prep, 2024a)

RAMSES uses MPI-based multiprocessing, which is inefficient at very large number of cores (~10,000). I am working on implementing OpenMP in RAMSES to construct hybrid paralleism, which greatly increases scalability of the code.

Velocity dispersion profiles of galaxies

Han et al. (in prep, 2023)

I used high-resolution zoom-in simulation of galaxies, NewHorizon, to measure the velocity dispersion profiles of galaixes and compare them with IFU observations.
